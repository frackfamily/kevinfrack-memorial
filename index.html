In Loving Memory

Rev. Kevin Frack

March 6, 1954  –  July 19, 2026

Pastor, farmer, missionary — a life devoted to Jesus, his family, and the land.

His Life

Reverend Kevin Frack peacefully stepped into the arms of Jesus at age 72 on July 19, 2026 surrounded by his family in his home in Mocksville, NC.

Kevin was born on March 6, 1954 at Keesler Air Force Base in Biloxi, MS. He was preceded in death by his father, Robert Frack and is survived by his mother Helen (Rissmiller). He was the oldest of 3 boys, leaving behind his brothers Jeff Frack (Linda) and Scott Frack (Jean). He completed his undergraduate education at Moravian College and went on to study Tropical Agriculture at the University of Florida. He served as an agricultural missionary in both Nicaragua and Honduras and later went on to complete Moravian Theological Seminary in Pennsylvania.

He served as pastor at Bruderheim Moravian Church in Bruderheim, Alberta, Canada; Church of the Redeemer in Columbus, Ohio; and Ardmore Moravian Church in Winston-Salem, NC. Kevin retired from the Moravian Church after 30 years in 2007 and went back into the agricultural mission field in Nicaragua. In 2009 he received a call to serve as the pastor of Cornerstone Christian Church in Mocksville, NC where he remained until 2026.

For the last 9 years Kevin and his wife of 47 years, Beth, have lived out their dream on their small farm in Mocksville. Kevin loved to be outside, work in the garden and share his love of farming with others. But even more than farming, Kevin loved and devoted his life to Jesus. His greatest desire was that others would come to know Jesus as their Lord and Savior.

Kevin leaves behind his two children Seth Frack (Whitney) and Serah Haley (Mat). He was the best dad imaginable and an even better “Pa” to his grandchildren: Nate, Jane, Anne, Eli, Lucas and Aylah.

Kevin’s family would like to thank their loving friends and family, Cornerstone Christian Church, Vine Branch, and Trellis Supportive Care for their generosity and all they did to support, carry and love Kevin through treatment and later into glory in the presence of Jesus.
